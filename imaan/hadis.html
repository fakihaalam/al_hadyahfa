حَدَّثَنَا وَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ
إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ
Indeed, actions are judged by intentions.
This means every action is rewarded or judged according to the sincerity of intention behind it.
- Sahih al-Bukhari
مَنْ غَشَّنَا فَلَيْسَ مِنَّا
Whoever cheats us is not one of us.
This highlights the prohibition of deceit and the importance of honesty in Islam.
- Sahih Muslim
الْمُسْلِمُ أَخُو الْمُسْلِمِ
A Muslim is a brother of another Muslim.
This underscores the value of unity and mutual support among Muslims.
- Sunan Abu Dawood
الدُّنْيَا سِجْنُ الْمُؤْمِنِ وَجَنَّةُ الْكَافِرِ
The world is a prison for the believer and a paradise for the disbeliever.
This explains the challenges faced by believers in this world compared to the Hereafter.
- Sahih Muslim
الْمَرْءُ مَعَ مَنْ أَحَبَّ
A person will be with those whom he loves.
This emphasizes the importance of loving righteous company to achieve good in the Hereafter.
- Sahih al-Bukhari
طَلَبُ الْعِلْمِ فَرِيضَةٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ
Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim.
This highlights the significance of acquiring knowledge as a fundamental duty in Islam.
- Sunan Ibn Majah
مَنْ صَمَتَ نَجَا
Whoever remains silent has been saved.
This advises maintaining silence to avoid unnecessary speech that could lead to sin.
- Sunan al-Tirmidhi
لَا يَرْحَمُ اللَّهُ مَنْ لَا يَرْحَمُ النَّاسَ
Allah does not show mercy to those who do not show mercy to people.
This emphasizes the importance of compassion and mercy towards others.
- Sahih al-Bukhari
مَنْ دَلَّ عَلَى خَيْرٍ فَلَهُ مِثْلُ أَجْرِ فَاعِلِهِ
Whoever guides someone to goodness will have a reward similar to the one who does it.
This encourages guiding others towards righteous deeds to share in their reward.
- Sahih Muslim
لَا يَحِلُّ لِمُسْلِمٍ أَنْ يَهْجُرَ أَخَاهُ فَوْقَ ثَلَاثِ لَيَالٍ
It is not lawful for a Muslim to forsake his brother beyond three nights.
This stresses the importance of reconciliation and maintaining relationships among Muslims.
- Sahih al-Bukhari
الرَّاحِمُونَ يَرْحَمُهُمُ الرَّحْمَنُ
The Merciful will show mercy to those who have mercy on others.
This highlights the divine reward for showing mercy and kindness to others.
- Sunan al-Tirmidhi
